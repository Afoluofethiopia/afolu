About AFOLU

Shaping Sustainable Land Use Systems

AFOLU Ethiopia operates at the intersection of agriculture, forestry, and land stewardship — driving evidence-based policy, community participation, and ecosystem restoration across Ethiopia's diverse landscapes. Established in 2019, the organization brings together technical expertise and a deep commitment to sustainable development from the highlands of Ras Dashen to the Dallol Depression.

As a nationally rooted organization aligned with Ethiopia's Climate Resilient Green Economy (CRGE) strategy, AFOLU works in close partnership with government agencies, research institutions, international development partners, and local communities. Its work contributes directly to national goals around food security, biodiversity conservation, carbon sequestration, and climate adaptation — ensuring that today's land use decisions protect the ecological inheritance of future generations.

About AFOLU Ethiopia

AFOLU Ethiopia was established to address the complex and interconnected challenges facing Ethiopia's agricultural, forestry, and land use sectors. The organization recognizes that sustainable development cannot be achieved through isolated interventions — it requires coordinated, science-led, and community-driven approaches that address the root causes of land degradation, food insecurity, and environmental vulnerability. From its founding, AFOLU has pursued an integrated model that links technical expertise with practical field implementation across the country's varied landscapes.

The name AFOLU itself — Agriculture, Forestry, and Other Land Use — reflects the breadth of the organization's mandate. It encompasses everything from crop science and agroforestry to watershed restoration, wildlife conservation, and climate finance mechanisms like REDD+. This wide scope allows AFOLU to identify and respond to the interdependencies between different land use systems, ensuring that solutions in one sector complement and reinforce progress in others. The organization operates with a deep understanding that forests, farms, and people are inseparable parts of the same ecological and social system.

At its core, AFOLU Ethiopia is driven by a belief that sustainable land management is both an environmental imperative and a social justice issue. The communities most dependent on Ethiopia's natural resources — smallholder farmers, pastoral communities, forest-dependent households — are also the most vulnerable to the consequences of land degradation and climate change. AFOLU's programs are therefore designed not just to restore landscapes, but to strengthen the livelihoods, resilience, and agency of the millions of Ethiopians whose futures are bound to the health of their land.

Mission & Vision

AFOLU Ethiopia's mission is to advance sustainable agriculture, forest conservation, and integrated land use management across Ethiopia through evidence-based advocacy, community participation, and climate-resilient development programs. The organization is committed to building systems that support food security, biodiversity conservation, and ecosystem restoration at scale. Every program, initiative, and partnership AFOLU undertakes is guided by this foundational commitment to sustainable, equitable, and science-led land stewardship.

The vision of AFOLU Ethiopia is a future in which Ethiopia's landscapes are ecologically healthy, productively managed, and resilient to the pressures of climate change and population growth. This vision is grounded in the recognition that Ethiopia's forests, soils, water systems, and biodiversity are not just environmental assets — they are the foundation of the nation's food security, economic stability, and cultural identity. AFOLU envisions a country where communities are empowered stewards of their natural heritage, supported by strong institutions, sound policies, and adequate resources.

Achieving this vision requires long-term commitment, cross-sectoral collaboration, and a willingness to work at multiple scales simultaneously — from the individual farm plot to the national policy arena. AFOLU Ethiopia's mission and vision are therefore not static declarations but living frameworks that evolve in response to new scientific knowledge, emerging climate realities, and the changing needs of the communities it serves. They serve as both a compass and a call to action for all who share the conviction that sustainable land use is essential to Ethiopia's future.

Strategic Objectives
  • Advance evidence-based sustainable land management policy across Ethiopia
  • Restore degraded landscapes through community-driven ecosystem rehabilitation
  • Build climate resilience in vulnerable agricultural and forest-dependent communities
  • Strengthen biodiversity conservation across priority ecosystem types
  • Improve rural livelihoods through integrated AFOLU value chain development
  • Contribute to Ethiopia's national climate commitments through AFOLU sector action
AFOLU Ethiopia's mission and vision serve as both a compass and a call to action — a living framework that evolves with new knowledge and realities while remaining anchored in the conviction that sustainable land use is essential to Ethiopia's future.

The organization's leaders are guided by a shared commitment to transparency, accountability, and collaborative decision-making. They represent Ethiopia's AFOLU sector at climate negotiations, regional conferences, and development dialogues, ensuring that the organization's field-based evidence informs broader conversations about land use, food systems, and climate action. Through this combination of internal coordination and external advocacy, AFOLU's leadership shapes not only the organization's own programs but contributes to the wider transformation of Ethiopia's land governance landscape.

Leadership at AFOLU is distributed, collaborative, and outward-looking — combining technical depth with policy advocacy to ensure that Ethiopia's sustainable land use agenda is championed at every level.

Organizational Structure

AFOLU Ethiopia is organized around eight specialized technical sectors, each focused on a distinct dimension of sustainable land use — from plant science and animal husbandry to forestry management and wildlife conservation. This sector-based structure allows the organization to develop deep expertise within each discipline while maintaining the flexibility to coordinate across sectors when addressing complex, landscape-scale challenges. Each sector is led by dedicated technical staff whose work is integrated through overarching program frameworks and shared strategic priorities.

Above the sector level, AFOLU's organizational structure includes program coordination units responsible for flagship initiatives such as the Green Legacy Initiative, REDD+ implementation, climate resilience programming, and food security interventions. These units bring together expertise from multiple sectors to design and deliver integrated programs that address the interconnected drivers of land degradation, climate vulnerability, and food insecurity. They also manage partnerships with government agencies, development organizations, and research institutions, ensuring that AFOLU's work is well-resourced and strategically aligned.

Supporting the technical and program units is an administrative and communications infrastructure that ensures operational efficiency, financial accountability, and effective knowledge sharing. This includes monitoring and evaluation systems, geospatial data management, resource mobilization functions, and stakeholder communication platforms. Together, these structural elements allow AFOLU Ethiopia to operate as a coherent, high-functioning organization capable of delivering impact at the scale that Ethiopia's land use challenges demand. The organizational structure is designed to be adaptive, evolving as the organization grows and as new opportunities and challenges emerge.

Plant Science

Plant science is one of the foundational pillars of AFOLU Ethiopia's technical work, encompassing research, development, and extension activities related to crop improvement, seed systems, agronomic practices, and plant health management. Ethiopia's agricultural productivity depends fundamentally on the availability of improved varieties, healthy planting materials, and evidence-based agronomic guidance for smallholder farmers. AFOLU's plant science work addresses these needs by linking scientific research with practical farm-level application across the country's diverse agroecological zones.

A central focus of AFOLU's plant science activities is the development and promotion of climate-resilient crop varieties capable of maintaining productivity under conditions of variable rainfall, rising temperatures, and shifting growing seasons. This work involves collaboration with national research institutes, international agricultural research centers, and university partners to evaluate and disseminate varieties that combine high yield potential with tolerance to drought, waterlogging, pests, and diseases. Seed system strengthening — including the development of community-based seed banks and certified seed supply chains — is another key component of this work.

Beyond variety development, AFOLU's plant science sector provides technical support for integrated soil fertility management, pest and disease control, precision agronomic practices, and post-harvest handling. These interventions are designed to improve crop yields, reduce production losses, and enhance the nutritional quality of food produced by smallholder households. By strengthening the plant science knowledge base available to farmers and extension agents, AFOLU contributes to a more productive, resilient, and sustainable agricultural system that underpins Ethiopia's broader food security and rural development goals.

Key Focus Areas

  • Community-based seed system strengthening and seed bank development

🌍 Sector 02

Natural Resource Management

Natural Resource Management is a cornerstone of AFOLU Ethiopia's operational mandate, addressing the conservation, sustainable use, and restoration of the land, water, forest, and biodiversity resources that underpin Ethiopia's ecological and economic systems. The sector recognizes that natural resources are not unlimited assets — they require active stewardship, science-based management, and community participation to remain productive and resilient in the face of growing pressures from population growth, agricultural expansion, and climate change. AFOLU's NRM work spans from micro-watershed rehabilitation to national-scale landscape restoration programming.

Integrated watershed management is one of the most critical components of AFOLU's NRM activities. Watersheds in Ethiopia are under severe pressure from soil erosion, deforestation, overgrazing, and unsustainable agricultural practices that degrade water quality, reduce groundwater recharge, and increase the frequency and severity of floods and droughts. AFOLU addresses these pressures through a combination of physical soil and water conservation structures, biological rehabilitation measures such as area closures and reforestation, and community-based watershed governance systems that give local communities the authority and capacity to manage their own natural resource base.

The NRM sector also plays a central role in AFOLU's climate change response, recognizing that healthy ecosystems are among the most powerful tools available for both mitigating greenhouse gas emissions and adapting to a changing climate. Restored forests sequester carbon, rehabilitated watersheds buffer communities against extreme weather, and well-managed soils store both water and nutrients more effectively. By investing in natural resource management as a climate strategy, AFOLU Ethiopia contributes simultaneously to national climate commitments, biodiversity conservation goals, and the long-term food security and livelihood resilience of rural communities across the country.

🌿 Sector 03

Agroforestry Systems

Agroforestry represents one of the most ancient and ecologically sophisticated approaches to land management in Ethiopia, integrating trees, crops, and livestock within the same farming landscape to produce multiple benefits simultaneously. AFOLU Ethiopia's agroforestry sector builds on this deep indigenous knowledge tradition while incorporating modern scientific understanding of how trees and agricultural systems interact to improve soil fertility, water retention, biodiversity, and household income. From the coffee forests of Kaffa to the parkland systems of Tigray, agroforestry takes many forms across Ethiopia's diverse agroecological zones, each adapted to local conditions and community needs.

The sector focuses on both promoting established agroforestry systems and developing new integrated approaches suited to emerging challenges such as climate variability, land scarcity, and market development. Key system types include homegarden agroforestry, which integrates diverse food and timber trees around homesteads to support year-round nutrition and income; parkland agroforestry, where scattered trees are retained and managed within cropping fields to improve soil fertility and microclimate; silvopastoral systems, which combine trees with pasture and livestock to improve rangeland productivity; and coffee-based forest systems that provide both conservation and high-value commodity production benefits.

AFOLU's agroforestry work is also closely linked to the organization's climate and land restoration goals. Agroforestry systems are among the most effective approaches for sequestering carbon, reducing soil erosion, restoring degraded landscapes, and building farmer resilience to climate shocks. By expanding the adoption of appropriate agroforestry practices among smallholder farmers and pastoral communities, AFOLU contributes to a more productive, biodiverse, and climate-resilient agricultural landscape — one that sustains both people and ecosystems across generations.

🐄 Sector 04

Animal Science

Animal science is a vital component of AFOLU Ethiopia's technical portfolio, addressing the health, productivity, and sustainable management of Ethiopia's livestock systems, which represent a cornerstone of rural livelihoods, food security, and national economic output. Ethiopia holds one of Africa's largest livestock populations, and the sector plays a critical role in providing food, income, draft power, and cultural value to millions of farming and pastoral households. AFOLU's animal science work focuses on improving livestock productivity, strengthening veterinary service delivery, and promoting sustainable rangeland and pasture management practices.

A major focus of the animal science sector is the development and dissemination of improved animal husbandry practices that enhance the health and productive performance of cattle, sheep, goats, poultry, and other livestock species. This includes work on animal nutrition, breed improvement, reproductive management, disease prevention, and value chain development for livestock products such as meat, milk, hides, and eggs. AFOLU also supports the strengthening of community-based animal health systems and the training of paraveterinary workers who can extend the reach of formal veterinary services to remote and underserved rural areas.

The animal science sector is also deeply engaged with the sustainable management of Ethiopia's rangelands and pastoral landscapes, which are under growing pressure from population growth, climate variability, and land use change. Overgrazing, bush encroachment, and the fragmentation of traditional pastoral mobility corridors are contributing to rangeland degradation across many parts of the country. AFOLU works with pastoral communities, regional governments, and research institutions to develop and promote rangeland management practices that balance livestock production needs with ecosystem health, biodiversity conservation, and long-term land productivity.

Key Areas of Work
  • Livestock breed improvement and reproductive management
  • Animal nutrition, health, and disease prevention programs
  • Community-based animal health worker training
  • Rangeland and pasture management for pastoral communities
  • Livestock product value chain development
  • Integration of livestock systems with crop and agroforestry practices

🏘 Sector 05

Rural Development

Rural development is the connective tissue of AFOLU Ethiopia's sector work, ensuring that technical knowledge and program resources reach the farming and pastoral communities who need them most. Ethiopia's rural population represents the majority of the country's citizens, and their ability to adopt improved land management practices, access markets, and respond to climate change depends heavily on the quality and accessibility of agricultural extension services, community organization, and rural infrastructure. AFOLU's rural development sector works to strengthen these enabling conditions across diverse rural landscapes.

Agricultural extension is a central component of AFOLU's rural development activities. Extension workers serve as the critical link between scientific knowledge and farmer practice, translating research findings into practical recommendations that can be applied in real farm conditions. AFOLU supports the training and deployment of extension agents across multiple sectors — from crop production and agroforestry to livestock management and natural resource conservation — ensuring that communities receive integrated technical support that reflects the complexity of their farming systems. Farmer field schools, demonstration plots, and community learning platforms are among the key extension methodologies employed.

Beyond extension, AFOLU's rural development work addresses broader issues of community empowerment, gender equity, and institutional capacity building. Effective rural development requires not just technical knowledge transfer but the strengthening of local institutions — farmers' cooperatives, water user associations, forest management groups, and watershed committees — that can sustain improved practices long after project support ends. AFOLU invests in the organizational capacity of these community groups, helping them to manage shared resources, resolve conflicts, access markets, and engage constructively with government and development partners in the planning and governance of their landscapes.

Extension Methodologies

  • Demonstration plots and technology showcase farms
  • Community-based facilitator training programs
  • Farmers' cooperative strengthening and market linkages
  • Gender-responsive rural development programming
  • Local institution capacity building and governance support
Rural development connects technical knowledge with community practice — strengthening the local institutions, social capital, and economic resources that enable farming and pastoral communities to manage their landscapes sustainably for generations.

📊 Sector 06

Agro-Economics & Value Chains

Agro-economics is the analytical and strategic backbone of AFOLU Ethiopia's development work, providing the market intelligence, value chain analysis, and economic frameworks necessary to translate ecological and agricultural improvements into sustainable livelihoods and income growth. Ethiopia's smallholder farmers and forest-dependent communities produce a wide range of valuable commodities — from coffee and honey to timber and livestock products — but their ability to capture fair value from these resources is often constrained by weak market linkages, limited access to finance, inadequate market information, and inefficient value chains. AFOLU's agro-economics sector works to address these constraints systematically.

Value chain development is a core focus of AFOLU's agro-economic work. This involves mapping the full journey of agricultural and forest products from production through processing, trade, and final consumption, identifying the bottlenecks and inefficiencies that reduce the returns available to primary producers. Based on this analysis, AFOLU designs interventions that strengthen producer organizations, improve post-harvest handling and processing, develop certification and quality assurance systems, and build more direct and transparent relationships between farmers and buyers. Special attention is given to high-value products associated with sustainable land management — such as organic coffee, sustainable timber, and non-timber forest products — where environmental stewardship and market premium can reinforce each other.

Agricultural finance and investment mobilization are also important dimensions of AFOLU's agro-economic work. Smallholder farmers often lack access to the credit, insurance, and savings products they need to invest in improved inputs, technologies, and infrastructure. AFOLU works with financial institutions, microfinance organizations, and government programs to design and promote financial products appropriate to the needs and risk profiles of rural households. By improving smallholders' access to financial services, AFOLU helps to unlock the investment capacity necessary to scale up sustainable agricultural and land management practices across Ethiopia's rural landscapes.

🌳 Sector 07

Forestry Management

Forestry is one of the most strategically important sectors within AFOLU Ethiopia's portfolio, given the central role that forests play in Ethiopia's climate system, biodiversity heritage, watershed stability, and rural livelihoods. Ethiopia's forests — including the ancient Afromontane forests of the southwest, the dry woodland ecosystems of the lowlands, and the remnant highland forests scattered across the country — are under severe and growing pressure from agricultural expansion, fuelwood extraction, illegal logging, and climate change. AFOLU's forestry sector works to reverse these trends through sustainable forest management, community-based conservation, landscape restoration, and carbon finance mechanisms.

Sustainable forest management is at the heart of AFOLU's forestry work, encompassing the planning, protection, and productive management of both natural forests and planted woodlots. This includes the development and implementation of forest management plans, the establishment of community forest management systems that give local communities legal authority over their forest resources, and the promotion of reduced-impact harvesting practices that allow forests to be used sustainably without degrading their ecological integrity. AFOLU also supports the development of timber and non-timber forest product value chains, helping forest-dependent communities to derive legitimate and sustainable income from their forest resources as an incentive for conservation.

The forestry sector is also the organizational home of AFOLU's work on forest carbon, including the implementation of REDD+ programs, the development of forest reference emission levels, and the strengthening of national forest monitoring systems. As climate finance mechanisms continue to evolve and expand, forests represent one of Ethiopia's most significant opportunities to attract international climate investment while simultaneously achieving national development goals. AFOLU's forestry team works at the interface of technical forest management, community engagement, and international climate policy to ensure that Ethiopia's forests deliver their full potential for carbon sequestration, biodiversity conservation, watershed protection, and community livelihood support.

  • Timber and non-timber forest product value chain development

  • Reforestation and assisted natural regeneration programs
  • Forest law enforcement and anti-illegal logging systems

🦁 Sector 08

Wildlife & Biodiversity Conservation

Wildlife and ecotourism conservation represents one of AFOLU Ethiopia's most ecologically significant areas of work, reflecting the organization's commitment to protecting the rich biodiversity that characterizes Ethiopia's diverse landscapes. Ethiopia is one of Africa's most biodiverse nations, home to a remarkable array of endemic species — including the Ethiopian wolf, the Gelada baboon, the Walia ibex, and hundreds of endemic bird species — as well as a diversity of forest, savanna, wetland, and alpine ecosystems that support both global biodiversity conservation priorities and local community livelihoods. AFOLU works to integrate biodiversity conservation within broader land use management frameworks, ensuring that wildlife habitat protection is treated as an integral component of sustainable landscape management.

Protected area management and landscape connectivity are key dimensions of AFOLU's wildlife conservation work. Ethiopia's national parks, wildlife reserves, and biosphere reserves provide critical refugia for endemic and endangered species, but their effectiveness depends on the quality of management, the engagement of surrounding communities, and the maintenance of ecological corridors that allow wildlife populations to move between protected areas. AFOLU works with the Ethiopian Wildlife Conservation Authority and regional conservation agencies to strengthen management systems, develop community conservation agreements, and design landscape-level connectivity plans that protect wildlife habitat while accommodating sustainable land use.

Ecotourism development is a central component of AFOLU's approach to making wildlife conservation economically viable and socially equitable for rural communities. When communities receive tangible economic benefits from wildlife — through tourism revenue, employment, and enterprise development — they have a strong incentive to protect and manage wildlife resources sustainably. AFOLU supports the development of community-based ecotourism enterprises, the training of local guides and hospitality workers, the improvement of tourism infrastructure in and around protected areas, and the marketing of Ethiopia's extraordinary natural heritage to domestic and international visitors.

🌱 Flagship Program

The Green Legacy Initiative

The Green Legacy Initiative stands as one of the most ambitious and symbolically powerful environmental restoration campaigns in Africa's recent history, mobilizing millions of Ethiopian citizens in a collective act of ecological renewal through mass tree planting and landscape restoration. Launched in 2019 under national leadership, the initiative has planted over 28 billion seedlings across Ethiopia's diverse regions, making it one of the largest tree planting campaigns ever recorded globally. AFOLU Ethiopia plays a central technical role in the initiative, supporting seedling production, species selection, site preparation, and post-planting survival monitoring across participating communities and landscapes.

The Green Legacy Initiative goes far beyond symbolic gestures of environmental concern — it is a strategically designed landscape restoration program targeting the most degraded and climate-vulnerable areas of the country. Priority planting sites are selected based on watershed function, soil erosion risk, biodiversity value, and community livelihood importance, ensuring that the ecological benefits of tree establishment are maximized and sustained. The species planted are carefully chosen to match local agroecological conditions, combining indigenous forest trees for long-term ecosystem restoration with multipurpose trees that provide immediate livelihood benefits such as fruit, fodder, timber, and fuelwood.

Beyond its ecological dimensions, the Green Legacy Initiative has become a powerful vehicle for national environmental education, civic engagement, and cultural reconnection with Ethiopia's forest heritage. By involving schools, community organizations, government institutions, and the general public in annual planting events, the initiative has helped to build a broad-based culture of environmental stewardship that extends far beyond the planted trees themselves. AFOLU Ethiopia sees the Green Legacy Initiative not as a one-time campaign but as the beginning of a long-term transformation in how Ethiopians relate to their land — a transformation rooted in pride, responsibility, and a shared vision of a green and resilient future.

Food Security & AFOLU Systems

Food security is both a fundamental human right and a complex development challenge that lies at the heart of AFOLU Ethiopia's mission. With a large rural population heavily dependent on rain-fed smallholder agriculture, Ethiopia's food security situation is acutely sensitive to fluctuations in rainfall, soil health, market conditions, and ecosystem services. AFOLU's food security program addresses these vulnerabilities through an integrated approach that combines improved agricultural production, natural resource management, agroforestry expansion, and value chain development to strengthen all four pillars of food security — availability, access, utilization, and stability — simultaneously.

Improving agricultural productivity at the farm level is a critical entry point for AFOLU's food security work. This involves the promotion of improved seed varieties, integrated soil fertility management, water harvesting and irrigation technologies, and climate-smart agronomic practices that help smallholder farmers produce more food per unit of land and labor, even under conditions of increasing climate stress. Agroforestry systems play a particularly important role in this regard, improving soil fertility, diversifying household food sources, reducing risk through production diversification, and providing valuable non-food products — timber, fodder, fuel — that free up household income for food purchases.

Ensuring that food security gains are sustained over time requires addressing the deeper structural vulnerabilities that make rural households susceptible to food crises — including land degradation, market failures, climate shocks, and governance weaknesses. AFOLU's food security program therefore integrates landscape restoration and watershed management as foundational food security investments, recognizing that the health of the ecological systems underlying agricultural production is ultimately the most important determinant of long-term food security. By combining immediate productivity support with long-term ecosystem restoration, AFOLU's approach builds a food security foundation that is both robust and resilient.

Climate Change & AFOLU

Climate resilience is a cross-cutting priority that runs through every dimension of AFOLU Ethiopia's work, reflecting the organization's recognition that climate change represents the most significant long-term threat to Ethiopia's agricultural systems, forest ecosystems, water resources, and rural livelihoods. Ethiopia's economy and food security are overwhelmingly dependent on rain-fed agriculture and natural resource systems that are acutely sensitive to changes in temperature, rainfall patterns, and the frequency of extreme weather events. Building resilience to these changes — through both adaptation measures that protect communities and mitigation actions that reduce the greenhouse gas emissions driving climate change — is therefore a central strategic priority for AFOLU.

AFOLU's climate resilience program is structured around two complementary pillars: landscape-scale ecosystem restoration and community-level adaptive capacity building. On the landscape side, the program invests in the rehabilitation of degraded watersheds, the expansion of forest cover through reforestation and agroforestry, and the improvement of soil and water conservation infrastructure that reduces the vulnerability of agricultural systems to drought and flood. These ecosystem-based adaptation approaches harness the natural buffering capacity of healthy landscapes to moderate the impacts of climate variability on farming communities, providing a cost-effective and sustainable complement to more conventional climate adaptation investments.

At the community level, AFOLU's climate resilience program works to strengthen the knowledge, institutions, and economic resources that enable rural households to anticipate, respond to, and recover from climate shocks. This includes the promotion of climate-smart agricultural practices tailored to local agroecological conditions, the development of early warning systems and climate information services, the strengthening of community-based disaster risk management systems, and the diversification of rural income sources to reduce dependence on climate-sensitive agricultural production. Together, these landscape and community-level investments build a climate resilience foundation that protects Ethiopia's rural population and natural resource base in the face of an increasingly uncertain climate future.

REDD+ Forest Carbon Management

REDD+ — Reducing Emissions from Deforestation and Forest Degradation — represents Ethiopia's most significant engagement with international climate finance mechanisms and one of AFOLU's most technically sophisticated program areas. As a developing country with substantial forest resources and significant deforestation pressures, Ethiopia is well positioned to benefit from REDD+ financing, which compensates countries for measurable reductions in forest-based greenhouse gas emissions relative to historical reference levels. AFOLU plays a central role in Ethiopia's REDD+ implementation, providing technical support for forest monitoring systems, participatory forest management programs, and the development of the policy and institutional frameworks necessary for effective REDD+ governance.

The technical foundation of Ethiopia's REDD+ program is a robust national forest monitoring system that uses satellite remote sensing, ground-based forest inventory data, and advanced carbon accounting methodologies to measure and report changes in forest cover and carbon stocks over time. AFOLU supports the development and maintenance of this monitoring system, working with national research institutions, regional forest agencies, and international technical partners to ensure that Ethiopia's forest carbon accounts are accurate, transparent, and consistent with international reporting standards. This technical credibility is essential for Ethiopia to access results-based climate finance and demonstrate its commitment to climate mitigation through forest conservation.

Beyond the technical dimensions of carbon monitoring, AFOLU's REDD+ work is deeply engaged with the human dimensions of forest conservation — the communities who live in and depend on Ethiopia's forests and whose behavior is the ultimate determinant of whether deforestation is reduced. Participatory forest management systems that give communities legal rights over their forest resources, share the benefits of conservation with local households, and build local capacity for sustainable forest management are at the center of AFOLU's REDD+ implementation strategy. By aligning the economic interests of forest communities with the conservation outcomes required for REDD+ financing, AFOLU seeks to create a self-sustaining system of forest governance that delivers climate, biodiversity, and livelihood benefits simultaneously.

Geospatial Monitoring & GIS

Geospatial technologies are among the most powerful tools available for monitoring, planning, and managing complex land use systems at landscape scale, and AFOLU Ethiopia has invested significantly in developing its capacity to apply these technologies in support of its program and policy work. Geographic Information Systems (GIS), satellite remote sensing, drone-based aerial imagery, and spatial modeling tools enable AFOLU to track changes in forest cover, agricultural land use, watershed condition, and biodiversity habitat across vast and diverse landscapes — providing the spatial evidence base essential for effective natural resource governance and climate reporting.

AFOLU's geospatial work supports a wide range of specific applications across its technical sectors. In the forestry and REDD+ area, satellite-derived forest cover maps and carbon stock estimates provide the quantitative foundation for Ethiopia's national forest monitoring system and climate finance reporting. In watershed management, digital elevation models and hydrological analysis tools are used to identify erosion hotspots, prioritize restoration investments, and design soil and water conservation infrastructure. In agriculture, remote sensing data on vegetation health, soil moisture, and crop phenology supports early warning systems, input supply planning, and food security monitoring across diverse agroecological zones.

The development of AFOLU's geospatial capacity involves both the acquisition and maintenance of appropriate hardware and software systems and the training and professional development of skilled geospatial analysts. AFOLU works closely with national institutions such as the Ethiopian Space Science and Geospatial Institute, regional environmental agencies, and international technical partners to build a nationally rooted geospatial capacity that can sustain and expand its work over time. By making its geospatial data products and analytical tools available through its resources section, AFOLU also contributes to building the broader geospatial knowledge infrastructure that Ethiopia's land sector needs for transparent, evidence-based governance.
