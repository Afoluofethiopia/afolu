Waa'ee AFOLU Itoophiyaa

AFOLU Itoophiyaa madda qoodinsa beekumsa biyyoolessaa ta'ee hojjeta dedicated to strengthening the transmission, exchange, and practical application of knowledge among stakeholders working across agriculture, forestry, and broader natural resource management sectors. Established in 2019, the initiative was created to serve as a collaborative bridge connecting researchers, universities, government institutions, development organizations, practitioners, students, and local communities in order to support evidence-informed decision-making and promote sustainable land-use practices across Ethiopia’s diverse ecological landscapes. Recognizing that effective management of agricultural systems and natural resources depends not only on technical innovation but also on accessible and well-coordinated information flows, AFOLU facilitates dialogue that integrates scientific research, policy experience, indigenous knowledge systems, and field-based lessons learned from across the country’s agro-ecological zones—from the highland environments surrounding Ras Dashen to the lowland ecosystems of the Dallol Depression. Through workshops, professional forums, collaborative learning networks, digital outreach platforms, and stakeholder engagement events, AFOLU promotes continuous exchange of ideas that strengthens institutional cooperation and improves the relevance and usability of research outputs for practitioners and decision-makers alike. Aligned with Ethiopia’s Climate Resilient Green Economy strategy and national environmental priorities, the platform supports communication pathways that link knowledge producers with knowledge users, ensuring that climate-resilient agriculture, forest landscape restoration, watershed management, biodiversity conservation, and sustainable land stewardship approaches are informed by both scientific evidence and community-based experience. At the same time, AFOLU plays an important role in strengthening interdisciplinary collaboration and professional networking by creating opportunities for early-career experts, students, and practitioners to engage with experienced specialists and contribute actively to national dialogue on environmental sustainability challenges. By encouraging inclusive participation across institutions and sectors, the platform supports shared learning that enhances technical capacity and strengthens coordination among actors responding to land degradation, climate variability, ecosystem loss, and rural livelihood pressures. Through its commitment to knowledge dissemination, partnership development, and collaborative learning, AFOLU of Ethiopia contributes to building a strong national culture of informed engagement in agriculture and natural resource management, ensuring that knowledge generated in research centers, universities, development programs, and community landscapes can be translated into practical solutions that support resilient ecosystems, productive agricultural systems, and sustainable livelihoods for present and future generations across Ethiopia.

About AFOLU Ethiopia

AFOLU of Ethiopia operates primarily as a national knowledge-sharing platform dedicated to strengthening collaboration, learning exchange, and professional dialogue among stakeholders working in agriculture, forestry, and broader natural resource management sectors. Established in 2019, the initiative was founded to support the transmission of evidence-based knowledge across institutions, practitioners, research ers, students, and community actors engaged in sustainable land-use systems throughout Ethiopia. Recognizing that effective environmental stewardship depends on access to reliable information and coordinated action, AFOLU facilitates the flow of scientific findings, field experiences, policy insights, and local knowledge across diverse ecological regions and institutional settings. From the highland forest ecosystems surrounding Ras Dashen to the dryland environments of the Dallol Depression, the platform promotes communication that strengthens shared understanding of sustainable land management challenges and opportunities. By encouraging dialogue between academic institutions, government agencies, development organizations, and grassroots practitioners, AFOLU supports informed decision-making and improves the application of research results in practical land-use interventions. Through workshops, digital platforms, technical forums, and collaborative learning networks, the organization helps ensure that knowledge generated in one location or sector becomes accessible and useful across the wider agriculture and natural resources community. In doing so, AFOLU contributes to building a culture of continuous learning and cooperation that strengthens Ethiopia’s capacity to manage its landscapes sustainably for present and future generations.

Aligned with Ethiopia’s Climate Resilient Green Economy strategy and national environmental priorities, AFOLU of Ethiopia serves as a bridge connecting knowledge producers and knowledge users across multiple levels of the land-use sector. The platform works to ensure that scientific research, policy developments, field experiences, and indigenous knowledge systems are shared effectively among stakeholders responsible for shaping agricultural productivity, forest conservation, watershed protection, and ecosystem restoration initiatives. By facilitating dialogue among universities, research centers, government institutions, civil society organizations, and development partners, AFOLU strengthens coordination and promotes integrated responses to climate change and land degradation challenges. The organization emphasizes translating technical evidence into accessible formats that support practitioners and decision-makers in designing locally appropriate solutions. At the same time, AFOLU creates opportunities for community perspectives and traditional land management practices to inform national-level discussions and professional learning environments. Through seminars, professional exchanges, collaborative publications, and stakeholder engagement forums, the platform enhances institutional learning while supporting the adoption of climate-resilient and environmentally responsible land-use practices across Ethiopia’s diverse agro-ecological zones. These efforts reinforce the role of shared knowledge as a foundation for sustainable development and strengthen cooperation among actors working toward common environmental goals.

In addition to serving as a national knowledge exchange hub, AFOLU of Ethiopia promotes professional networking and capacity development opportunities that empower students, researchers, practitioners, and policymakers engaged in agriculture and natural resource management. The platform supports the dissemination of best practices, lessons learned from field experiences, and emerging research findings through structured communication channels designed to reach diverse audiences across sectors and regions. By encouraging interdisciplinary collaboration and continuous professional interaction, AFOLU helps strengthen the technical capacity of stakeholders working to address complex land-use challenges such as climate variability, soil degradation, deforestation, biodiversity loss, and watershed instability. The organization also plays an important role in supporting early-career professionals by creating accessible learning environments where they can engage with experienced experts and participate in national dialogue processes. Through its commitment to inclusive knowledge transmission and collaborative learning, AFOLU contributes to strengthening Ethiopia’s collective ability to manage agricultural landscapes and natural resources more effectively. Guided by the principle that sustainable land stewardship depends on informed participation and shared understanding, AFOLU of Ethiopia continues to expand partnerships that promote knowledge exchange as a central pillar of environmental resilience and long-term development across the country.

Our Core Pillars
  • Sustainable agriculture and food system strengthening
  • Forest conservation and landscape restoration
  • Watershed rehabilitation and water resource management
  • Climate change mitigation and adaptation
  • Biodiversity protection and wildlife conservation
  • Rural livelihood development and community empowerment
  • Evidence-based policy advocacy and knowledge sharing
AFOLU Ethiopia represents a commitment to ensuring that today's land use decisions protect the ecological inheritance of future generations — bridging science, policy, and community action across Ethiopia's diverse landscapes.

Mission & Vision

AFOLU Ethiopia's mission is to advance sustainable agriculture, forest conservation, and integrated land use management across Ethiopia through evidence-based advocacy, community participation, and climate-resilient development programs.

The vision of AFOLU Ethiopia is a future in which Ethiopia's landscapes are ecologically healthy, productively managed, and resilient to the pressures of climate change and population growth — a future in which every community has access to the natural resources they need to thrive.

Strategic Objectives
  • Advance evidence-based sustainable land management policy across Ethiopia
  • Restore degraded landscapes through community-driven ecosystem rehabilitation
  • Build climate resilience in vulnerable agricultural and forest-dependent communities
  • Strengthen biodiversity conservation across priority ecosystem types
  • Improve rural livelihoods through integrated AFOLU value chain development
  • Contribute to Ethiopia's national climate commitments through AFOLU sector action
AFOLU Ethiopia's mission and vision serve as both a compass and a call to action — a living framework anchored in the conviction that sustainable land use is essential to Ethiopia's ecological and social future.

🌾 Sector 01

Plant Science

The Plant Science sector at AFOLU Ethiopia addresses the full spectrum of crop development, seed system management, and agronomic innovation required to sustainably improve food production across Ethiopia's diverse agroecological zones. With farmers operating across highland, midland, and lowland environments, plant science interventions must be adapted, evidence-based, and locally relevant.

AFOLU's plant science work focuses on supporting the development and dissemination of improved crop varieties, strengthening formal and informal seed systems, and advancing knowledge on soil-crop-water interactions that can improve productivity while protecting long-term soil health.

Key Activities
  • Crop variety development, testing, and dissemination
  • Seed system strengthening — formal and community-based
  • Agronomic research and extension in diverse agroecological zones
  • Integrated crop management including pest and disease control
  • Climate-smart crop systems and drought-tolerant variety promotion

🌍 Sector 02

Natural Resource Management

Natural Resource Management (NRM) is a cornerstone of AFOLU Ethiopia's technical programming. Ethiopia's landscapes — from the Blue Nile gorges to the Rift Valley lakes — face significant pressure from land degradation, deforestation, and water scarcity. Effective NRM addresses these pressures through a combination of physical conservation measures, community-based governance systems, and ecosystem restoration.

AFOLU's NRM sector leads integrated watershed management programs that combine soil and water conservation infrastructure with community organization, land use planning, and long-term monitoring. The goal is not only to stop degradation but to actively rehabilitate the ecological functions that support agriculture, water supply, and biodiversity.

Key Areas
  • Integrated watershed management and rehabilitation
  • Soil and water conservation structures and practices
  • Community-based NRM governance and planning
  • Land use and land cover monitoring
  • Rangeland and dryland ecosystem management

🌿 Sector 03

Agroforestirii

Agroforestry represents one of the most powerful tools available for simultaneously addressing food security, climate resilience, and ecological restoration. By integrating trees, crops, and livestock on the same land unit, agroforestry systems can improve smallholder incomes, protect soil and water resources, sequester carbon, and enhance biodiversity — all while producing food and fodder for household and market needs.

AFOLU Ethiopia's agroforestry sector promotes evidence-based agroforestry practices adapted to Ethiopia's diverse farming systems — from homestead gardens in the highlands to parkland agroforestry in the drylands. The sector works with farmers, communities, and local institutions to design, plant, and manage agroforestry systems that deliver lasting benefits.

Key Activities
  • Agroforestry system design and farmer participatory planning
  • Multipurpose tree species selection, nursery, and planting
  • Integration of trees into crop and livestock farming systems
  • Farmer training and peer-learning networks
  • Carbon benefit assessment and climate reporting

🐄 Sector 04

Animal Science

Livestock production is central to the livelihoods of millions of Ethiopian farmers, pastoralists, and agropastoralists. Ethiopia holds Africa's largest livestock population, yet productivity remains constrained by disease, poor nutrition, limited market access, and the increasing pressure of climate variability on rangelands and pasture systems.

AFOLU's animal science activities focus on improving livestock productivity while managing the land use pressures that high livestock densities can create — including overgrazing, rangeland degradation, and watershed damage. The organization approaches livestock development through an integrated lens that links productivity improvement with ecological sustainability.

Key Areas
  • Livestock breed improvement and genetic resource conservation
  • Animal nutrition and feed system development
  • Veterinary services and disease control
  • Rangeland and pasture management
  • Livestock value chain and market development

🏘 Sector 05

Rural Development

Rural development and agricultural extension form the bridge between technical knowledge and farmer practice in AFOLU Ethiopia's programming. Extension services bring improved technologies, practices, and market connections directly to smallholder farmers and rural communities across Ethiopia's diverse regions.

AFOLU's extension and rural development activities are guided by a participatory approach that begins with community needs assessment, builds local technical capacity through farmer-to-farmer learning platforms, and connects rural communities to input markets, financial services, and agricultural value chains.

Extension Approach
  • Farmer Training Centers and demonstration sites
  • Farmer-to-farmer learning networks
  • Women and youth farmer group empowerment
  • Agricultural market linkage and value chain development
  • Digital extension tools and rural ICT platforms

📊 Sector 06

Agro-Economics

The agro-economics sector provides the analytical and strategic foundation for AFOLU Ethiopia's value chain, market, and policy work. By understanding the economic drivers and constraints shaping agricultural and land use decisions, AFOLU can design interventions that create real and lasting incentives for sustainable practice adoption.

Ethiopia's agricultural sector is characterized by predominant smallholder farming, fragmented markets, limited financial services, and significant post-harvest losses that reduce economic returns to sustainable land management investment. AFOLU's agro-economics work addresses these structural challenges through targeted value chain analysis, market systems development, and agricultural policy advocacy.

Core Activities
  • Agricultural value chain analysis and development
  • Market systems diagnosis and market development programming
  • Agricultural policy analysis and evidence-based advocacy
  • Rural financial services and agricultural credit facilitation
  • Economic valuation of ecosystem services

🌳 Sector 07

Marga

Ethiopia's forests are among its most valuable and most threatened natural assets. Covering diverse forest types from the Afromontane forests of the highlands to the dry woodland systems of the lowlands, Ethiopia's forests provide critical ecosystem services — including water regulation, biodiversity habitat, and carbon storage — while supporting the livelihoods of millions of forest-dependent communities.

AFOLU Ethiopia's forestry sector works to reverse the trend of forest loss by strengthening forest governance, building community forest management capacity, promoting sustainable timber and non-timber forest product value chains, and integrating forests into Ethiopia's climate change response through REDD+ and other mechanisms.

Key Activities
  • Community Forest Management (CFM) program development
  • Forest inventory and monitoring systems
  • REDD+ readiness and implementation support
  • Sustainable timber and non-timber forest product value chains
  • Forest landscape restoration planning

🦁 Sector 08

Wildlife & Ecotourism

Ethiopia is one of Africa's most biodiverse countries, with high levels of endemic species across its diverse ecosystems — from the Ethiopian wolf of the Bale Mountains to the gelada baboons of the Simien highlands. AFOLU Ethiopia's wildlife and ecotourism sector works to protect this biodiversity heritage while creating economic opportunities for communities living alongside wildlife.

The sector supports protected area management, human-wildlife conflict mitigation, community conservation areas, and ecotourism development as tools for simultaneously achieving biodiversity conservation and rural livelihood improvement — demonstrating that nature and communities can thrive together.

Key Focus Areas
  • Protected area management support and capacity building
  • Human-wildlife conflict mitigation programs
  • Community conservation area development
  • Wildlife population monitoring and research
  • Ecotourism product and enterprise development

The Sagantaa Dhaalota Magariisa is Ethiopia's most ambitious and high-profile environmental restoration program. Launched in 2019, the initiative set out to plant billions of seedlings across Ethiopia to restore degraded landscapes, reverse deforestation, and contribute to the global fight against climate change. In its first year alone, Ethiopia planted over 350 million trees in a single day — setting a world record.

Since then, the initiative has continued expanding, reaching over 28 billion seedlings planted across all of Ethiopia's regions. The program is uniquely community-driven, mobilizing millions of Ethiopians — from schoolchildren and farmers to government officials — in large-scale planting events that combine ecological restoration with national civic engagement.

Core Objectives
  • Restore degraded highlands, watersheds, and forest landscapes
  • Increase Ethiopia's national tree cover and forest area
  • Contribute to Ethiopia's Nationally Determined Contribution (NDC) under the Paris Agreement
  • Mobilize communities as active stewards of their natural environments
  • Support biodiversity recovery across restored landscapes
The Sagantaa Dhaalota Magariisa stands as proof that large-scale ecological transformation is possible when governments, communities, and citizens unite around a shared vision for sustainable landscapes.

Food Security

Food security remains one of Ethiopia's most pressing development challenges. Despite significant agricultural potential, millions of Ethiopians face seasonal food insecurity driven by drought, land degradation, poor market access, and climate variability. AFOLU Ethiopia's food security program addresses these challenges through integrated, multi-sector interventions that simultaneously improve agricultural production, natural resource management, and market linkages.

By integrating AFOLU's technical sectors — including plant science, animal science, agroforestry, and rural development — the food security program builds resilient food systems that can withstand both environmental and economic shocks. The program particularly focuses on the most vulnerable communities, including female-headed households and smallholder farmers in climate-stressed regions.

Program Pillars
  • Improved agricultural technology and input access for smallholders
  • Natural resource rehabilitation to restore productive land base
  • Market linkage and post-harvest loss reduction
  • Nutrition-sensitive agriculture and dietary diversification
  • Safety net and social protection integration

🌡 Program

Climate Resilience

Climate change poses an existential threat to Ethiopia's agricultural systems, water resources, and ecosystems. Rising temperatures, erratic rainfall, and increasing frequency of extreme weather events are already affecting millions of Ethiopians — particularly in climate-exposed lowlands and highlands. AFOLU Ethiopia's climate resilience program builds adaptive capacity at community, institutional, and landscape levels.

The program is aligned with Ethiopia's Climate Resilient Green Economy (CRGE) strategy, supporting implementation of nationally determined contributions through AFOLU sector actions. From climate-smart agriculture and ecosystem-based adaptation to community early warning systems and climate finance mechanisms, AFOLU's climate resilience program offers a comprehensive response.

Key Interventions
  • Climate-smart agriculture (CSA) technology dissemination
  • Ecosystem-based adaptation through watershed and landscape restoration
  • Community climate vulnerability assessments and adaptation planning
  • CRGE strategy implementation support at regional and local levels
  • Climate finance access facilitation for community-level programs
Resilience is not simply the capacity to survive shocks — it is the capacity to transform challenges into opportunities for regeneration. That is the vision animating AFOLU's climate resilience work.

🌳 Program

REDD+ Program

REDD+ (Reducing Emissions from Deforestation and Forest Degradation) represents a major international climate finance mechanism that rewards developing countries for protecting and restoring their forests. Ethiopia's forests store vast quantities of carbon, and their protection is critical both for global climate stability and for local communities that depend on forest ecosystem services.

AFOLU Ethiopia supports Ethiopia's REDD+ strategy by developing community-based forest management programs, building the forest monitoring and measurement systems required for REDD+ reporting, and linking forest conservation with biodiversity protection and community livelihood improvement through payment for ecosystem services approaches.

REDD+ Activities
  • REDD+ readiness support and national strategy implementation
  • Forest reference level development and emissions accounting
  • Community-based forest management linked to REDD+ incentives
  • Forest monitoring, reporting, and verification (MRV) systems
  • Safeguard information systems development

Geospatial technologies and Geographic Information Systems (GIS) are essential tools for AFOLU Ethiopia's monitoring, planning, and reporting functions. From satellite-based land cover change analysis to drone surveys and mobile data collection, AFOLU's geospatial unit deploys a range of remote sensing and spatial analysis capabilities to support evidence-based programming.

The unit supports forest monitoring for REDD+ and Green Legacy reporting, watershed characterization for NRM programming, and land use change analysis for policy and planning. By combining satellite imagery, field data, and spatial modeling, AFOLU produces high-quality geospatial products that inform both programmatic decisions and national policy processes.

Geospatial Capabilities
  • Land use and land cover change analysis using satellite imagery
  • Forest cover monitoring for REDD+ and Green Legacy
  • Watershed delineation and hydrological modeling
  • Mobile data collection and field survey management
  • Spatial data management and GIS platform development

Regional Pirojektii

AFOLU Ethiopia's regional projects address landscape-specific challenges and opportunities across Ethiopia's diverse administrative regions.   • Somali & Afar — Pastoral rangeland management and water development
